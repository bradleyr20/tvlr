Channels and Subchannels

Each published piece of content must have at least one primary channel. Content can have additional channels, but primary channel ist dominant and determinative as to display rules, content bucketing, and rendering logic (depending on use case).

NB: Channel and Subchannel names are subject to change.

Channels is a controlled and finite set.

Subchannels is a controlled but infinite set. There is no set number. They can be created by editorial staff at will, but there is a managed process for this which requires approvals. Software should control for duplication, ie through autocomplete, in order to keep data as clean as possible.

Subchannels will be loosely associated with channels, but not restrictively so. They are ontological rather than taxonomic. For example, a story tagged with the “Architecture” keyword might be assigned to primary channel “Arts & Culture”, or it might be assigned to primary channel “Style & Design”.
